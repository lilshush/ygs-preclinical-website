As Author
Oral Drug Delivery Author

Novel Glucagon-like Peptide-1 Analog Delivered Orally Reduces Postprandial Glucose Excursions in Porcine and Canine Models

Eldor R, Kidron M, Greenberg-Shushlav Y, Arbit E.

J Diabetes Sci Technol. 2010;4(6). (opens in a new tab)

This study directly validates the endoscopy-guided intragastric oral delivery approach in porcine and canine models. An orally delivered GLP-1/exenatide analog reduced postprandial glucose area-under-curve by up to 43% in pigs and 29–43% in dogs versus controls, demonstrating that orally delivered GLP-1 retains meaningful biological activity when administered via targeted intragastric route.

Oral Drug Delivery Author

Additive Effect of Oral Insulin and an Oral GLP-1 Agonist on Postprandial Glucose Excursions in a Porcine Model

Kidron M, Greenberg-Shushlav Y, Eldor R, Arbit E.

Endocrinol Diabetes Metab J. 2017;1(3):1–4.

Investigates concomitant pre-prandial delivery of enteric-coated oral insulin (ORMD-0801) and oral exenatide (ORMD-0901) capsules in pigs via endoscopy-guided duodenal administration. Combined delivery produced a robust additive hypoglycemic effect — a >50% drop below baseline glucose values — significantly greater than either drug alone, supporting the potential of an oral insulin + GLP-1 combination therapy.

Oral Drug Delivery Author

Recombinant Protease Inhibitor Enhances Oral Insulin Pharmacodynamics in Pigs

Kidron M, Greenberg-Shushlav Y, Arbit E.

Oramed Pharmaceuticals. 2018. (Conference poster)

Reports the development and in-vivo validation of a recombinant protease inhibitor (rPI) for the ORMD-0801 oral insulin formulation. The rPI demonstrated >2.5-fold higher anti-proteolytic activity than the naturally sourced counterpart and enhanced glucose-lowering pharmacodynamics in fasting pigs, supporting its use as a scalable excipient for oral insulin delivery.

Vascular Biology Acknowledged Contributor

Dasatinib Induces Endothelial Dysfunction Leading to Impaired Recovery from Ischaemia

Gover-Proaktor A, Leshem-Lev D, Winograd-Katz S, Partouche S, Samara A, Shapira S, Nardi-Agmon I, Harari E, Younis A, Najjar A, Kornowski R, Geiger B, Raanani P, Leader A, Granot G.

Br J Haematol. 2024;205(3):1011–1016. (opens in a new tab)

Dr. Greenberg Shushlav is acknowledged for performing the femoral artery ligation procedures used to model ischaemia in this study, applying her expertise in in-vivo microsurgical techniques. The research establishes a mechanistic link between dasatinib (a CML tyrosine kinase inhibitor) and endothelial dysfunction, with implications for vascular adverse events in oncology patients.

Regenerative Medicine Acknowledged Contributor

Encapsulation of Human-Bone-Marrow-Derived Mesenchymal Stem Cells in Small Alginate Beads Using One-Step Emulsification by Internal Gelation: In Vitro, and In Vivo Evaluation in Degenerate Intervertebral Disc Model

Sivan SS, Bonstein I, Marmor YN, Pelled G, Gazit Z, Amit M.

Pharmaceutics. 2022;14(6):1179. (opens in a new tab)

Dr. Greenberg Shushlav contributed to the in-vivo preclinical model design and execution for this study, which evaluates a cell delivery platform for regenerative spine therapy. Encapsulated mesenchymal stem cells achieved approximately 81% viability at two weeks in vitro, with biocompatible implantation sustained for eight weeks in a degenerate intervertebral disc animal model.
