Scientific Principle

3Rs & Model Refinement

The 3Rs are not a compliance obligation. They are the foundation of better preclinical science. Better model design, more precise procedures, and well-reasoned species selection produce cleaner data, reduce variability, and improve translational relevance.

Refinement

Procedures That Respect the Animal

Refined procedures minimize pain, distress, and unnecessary physiological disruption. This is not just welfare - it is also better science. Stress-induced variability confounds results. Welfare-conscious protocols produce more reproducible, interpretable data.

Reduction

Statistical Rigour From the Start

Proper power calculations, robust study design, and pilot-informed sample sizing reduce the number of animals needed without sacrificing statistical validity. Reduction starts at the design stage, not as an afterthought during protocol review.

Replacement

Choosing the Right Level of Evidence

Where in-vitro or ex-vivo platforms can answer the scientific question without animal use, this should be the default. YGS Preclinical actively evaluates whether in-vivo models are the appropriate tool for the specific question being asked.

Translational Relevance

Models That Predict Human Outcomes

The most important dimension of model refinement is relevance: does the model reflect the human biology being targeted? Poorly chosen models produce data that misleads rather than informs. Model relevance review is integrated into every study design.

Better welfare + better procedures + better model choice = better data and stronger translational value. The 3Rs and scientific quality are not in tension - they are the same goal.

About

Yael Greenberg Shushlav, DVM, DIP, LAM, MBA

Yael Greenberg Shushlav is a veterinarian and preclinical research specialist with over 25 years of hands-on experience across the full spectrum of laboratory animal species, including mouse, rat, rabbit, dog, goat, sheep, and swine. Her career spans academic collaboration, pharmaceutical CRO research, and independent preclinical consulting, covering a broad range of models and study types.

She holds a DVM from the Hebrew University of Jerusalem, an MBA from Derby University, and a Diploma in Laboratory Animal Medicine from Israel's Ministry of Agriculture. Her scientific training is complemented by deep procedural expertise - endoscopy, surgery, and microsurgery are all part of her active practice.

Her published work includes a study on a novel orally-delivered GLP-1 analog in porcine and canine models, directly validating her endoscopy-guided oral delivery approach, and a study on bone marrow cell engineering for transplant tolerance. At ProChon Biotech, she designed and executed in-vivo models in bone and cartilage tissue engineering, with protocols approved by the FDA for pre-IND submissions.

YGS Preclinical was established to provide the kind of specialized, expert-led preclinical support that early-stage programs need - scientifically grounded, practically executed, and genuinely focused on getting the model and the study design right.
